Manitoba Common Law Notice Can Exceed the ESA Maximum Significantly

Manitoba courts apply the Bardal factors (age, years of service, position, availability of comparable work) to determine reasonable notice under common law. Long-service or senior employees regularly receive awards of 12 to 18 months — far more than the 8-week ESA maximum. Always obtain a legal opinion before accepting any settlement offer.

Manitoba Severance Pay — Common Questions

How is Manitoba's minimum service period different?

Manitoba has one of the shortest minimum service periods in Canada — just 30 days. This means employees qualify for notice pay very quickly compared to provinces requiring 3 or 6 months.

Does Manitoba have group termination rules?

Yes. When 50 or more employees are terminated within a 4-week period, Manitoba requires additional advance notice to the Minister of Labour. Affected employees may be entitled to additional compensation.

Is there a cap on common law notice in Manitoba?

There is no hard cap on common law notice, though courts rarely exceed 24 months. The actual amount depends on your individual Bardal factors — use our calculator as a starting estimate.

Can I waive my ESA rights in Manitoba?

No. Manitoba's Employment Standards Code rights cannot be waived — any agreement that provides less than the ESA minimum is void. Common law rights, however, can be waived if you sign a release after legal advice.
