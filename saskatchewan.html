Common Law Notice in Saskatchewan Can Be Much More Than the ESA Minimum

Saskatchewan courts apply the Bardal factors to determine reasonable notice under common law. Employees in agriculture, energy, mining, and other Saskatchewan industries with significant tenure or senior roles can receive common law awards well beyond the 8-week ESA cap. Saskatchewan Severance Pay — Common Questions

Does Saskatchewan's law cover agricultural workers?

Agricultural employees in Saskatchewan may have different or limited protections under the Saskatchewan Employment Act. If you work in agriculture, consult the Ministry of Labour or an employment lawyer to confirm your specific rights.

What is the 13-week minimum service period about?

Saskatchewan requires 13 consecutive weeks of employment before you qualify for termination pay. This is slightly longer than most provinces, which typically use 3 months.

Can my employer give working notice instead of pay?

Yes. Your employer can provide working notice (you remain employed and working during the notice period) instead of pay in lieu. Both are valid ways of meeting the ESA obligation.

Are there special rules for mass layoffs in Saskatchewan?

Yes. When employers lay off 10 or more employees in a 4-week period, they must give additional advance notice to the Minister of Labour. These group termination rules provide additional protection.
