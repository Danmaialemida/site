PEI Severance Pay — Common Questions

Who regulates employment standards in PEI?

The Employment Standards Division within the Department of Economic Development, Innovation and Trade administers the PEI Employment Standards Act. Complaints about termination pay can be filed with this division.

Does PEI have seasonal employee protections?

PEI has significant seasonal employment in tourism, agriculture, and fisheries. Seasonal employees may have special considerations regarding their employment relationship and termination rights. Consult an employment lawyer for seasonal employment situations.

Is there a statute of limitations for wrongful dismissal in PEI?

Yes. There are time limits for making claims under the Employment Standards Act and for civil wrongful dismissal actions. Generally, you should seek legal advice promptly — ideally within 30 days of termination.

Can I get more than the ESA minimum in PEI?

Yes — through common law notice. PEI courts consider the Bardal factors and can award notice periods of 12 months or more for senior, long-service employees. Use our calculator to estimate your common law range.
