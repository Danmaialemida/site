Yukon Common Law Notice Can Be Much Higher Than the Statutory Minimum

Yukon follows Canadian common law principles, and courts in the territory apply the Bardal factors to determine reasonable notice. Employees in Whitehorse and across the Yukon who held senior roles or have long service are often entitled to 12 months or more of common law notice — significantly exceeding the 6-week ESA cap. Yukon Severance Pay — Common Questions

Does the Yukon Employment Standards Act cover all workers?

Most employees working in Yukon are covered by territorial employment standards. However, employees working for federally regulated employers (banks, airlines, telecoms) are covered by the Canada Labour Code regardless of where they work.

What industries are common in Yukon termination claims?

Mining, government, construction, tourism, and healthcare are among the most common industries in Yukon. Employees in management and technical roles in these sectors frequently have strong common law notice claims.

Can I file a complaint about wrongful dismissal in Yukon?

Yes. Complaints about violations of the Employment Standards Act can be filed with the Employment Standards Branch. For common law claims, you would need to pursue a civil action in Yukon courts.

Are there lawyers in Yukon specializing in employment law?

Yes. Employment lawyers in Whitehorse handle wrongful dismissal and severance pay disputes. Many employment lawyers also provide remote consultations for clients in rural Yukon. Free consultations are common.
