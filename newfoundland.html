NL Common Law Entitlements Can Greatly Exceed the 6-Week ESA Maximum

Newfoundland courts apply the Bardal factors and regularly award notice periods significantly higher than the statutory maximum. Employees in the offshore oil and gas industry, fisheries, mining, and government sectors have successfully claimed substantial common law notice. The ESA 6-week cap is merely the legal floor — your actual entitlement may be much higher.

Newfoundland Severance Pay — Common Questions

Why is Newfoundland's maximum lower than other provinces?

Newfoundland's Labour Standards Act has a statutory cap of 6 weeks — lower than the 8-week cap in most provinces. This makes common law notice even more important, as courts regularly award amounts far exceeding this limit.

Are offshore workers covered by NL labour standards?

Offshore oil workers on the Grand Banks may be covered by federal legislation or provincial legislation depending on their specific employment situation. Consult an employment lawyer familiar with the offshore industry for specific advice.

What if I was in a seasonal role?

Seasonal workers in Newfoundland may have different entitlements depending on the continuity of their employment relationship. If you were repeatedly rehired each season, courts may consider this in calculating your total service length.

Who enforces labour standards in Newfoundland and Labrador?

The Labour Relations Agency administers the Labour Standards Act in Newfoundland and Labrador. Complaints about improper termination can be filed with the Agency.
