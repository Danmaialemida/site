Nunavut Common Law Notice Can Exceed the Statutory Minimum

Nunavut follows Canadian common law principles. Employees in Iqaluit and across the territory — particularly those in government, mining, and construction — may have strong common law notice claims well above the statutory cap. Given the unique employment market in Nunavut (limited comparable positions, remote location), courts may award additional notice based on the difficulty of finding equivalent work. Always seek legal advice before signing any release.

Nunavut Severance Pay — Common Questions

Who administers labour standards in Nunavut?

The Labour Standards Office of Nunavut (nu-lsco.ca) administers the Labour Standards Act. For complaints about termination pay violations, contact the Labour Standards Office directly.

Does Nunavut's remote location affect common law notice?

Potentially yes. Courts consider the availability of comparable work when determining reasonable notice. In Nunavut, where comparable job opportunities may be limited, this factor could support longer notice periods under common law.

Are Inuit employees covered by the same standards?

Yes. The Labour Standards Act applies to all employees working in Nunavut regardless of background. However, Nunavummi Maligaliit (Nunavut Lands Claims Agreement) and other agreements may create additional rights for certain employees.

How can I access an employment lawyer in Nunavut?

Many employment lawyers in Ontario, Alberta, and BC provide remote consultations and can advise Nunavut employees on their rights. Legal Aid in Nunavut may also provide assistance in some cases.
