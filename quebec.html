In Quebec, the statutory requirement is advance notice of termination (or pay in lieu), not "severance pay" per se. Employees with 2 or more years of service also have protection against dismissal without good and sufficient cause under s. 124 of the Act.

Quebec Employees Have Additional Protection Under Section 124

Employees with 2+ years of continuous service in Quebec can file a complaint for dismissal without good and sufficient cause under s. 124 of the Act Respecting Labour Standards. This is distinct from common law wrongful dismissal and can result in reinstatement or additional compensation. Common law notice under Quebec Civil Law also provides additional protection beyond the statutory minimum.

Quebec Severance Pay — Common Questions

Does Quebec have severance pay or just notice?

Quebec requires advance notice of termination (or pay in lieu). Employees with 2+ years of service also have special protection against dismissal without good and sufficient cause under s.124 of the Act Respecting Labour Standards.

Can I file a complaint with the CNESST?

Yes. The Commission des normes, de l'équité, de la santé et de la sécurité du travail (CNESST) administers labour standards in Quebec. You can file a complaint if you were not given proper notice or pay in lieu.

How is Quebec different from other provinces?

Quebec uses Civil Law (not Common Law) as its legal foundation. This creates a distinct framework for employment disputes. However, courts in Quebec still consider similar factors when assessing reasonable notice periods.

Is bilingual notice required in Quebec?

Employment documents in Quebec must generally be provided in French under the Charter of the French Language. A termination notice should be in French, though your employment contract may specify other arrangements.
