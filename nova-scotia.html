Nova Scotia Common Law Notice Often Exceeds the ESA Minimum

Nova Scotia courts apply common law notice principles that can result in awards substantially higher than the ESA maximum. Long-service employees, senior managers, and employees in specialized roles regularly receive 12 to 18 months of notice under common law. Consult an employment lawyer in Halifax or anywhere in Nova Scotia before signing any termination document.

Nova Scotia Severance Pay — Common Questions

Who administers labour standards in Nova Scotia?

The Nova Scotia Department of Labour, Skills and Immigration administers the Labour Standards Code. Complaints about wrongful termination can be filed with the Labour Standards Division.

Does Nova Scotia have group termination requirements?

Yes. Nova Scotia requires additional notice when 10 or more employees are terminated within a 4-week period. The notice period varies based on the number of employees affected.

What if I was given inadequate notice?

If your employer gave you less notice than required by the Labour Standards Code, you can file a complaint with the Labour Standards Division. You may also have a common law claim for additional notice through the courts.

Can Nova Scotia employers offer pay in lieu of notice?

Yes. Employers can pay you your regular wages for the notice period instead of requiring you to work during that time. This is called "pay in lieu of notice" and is equivalent to working notice for ESA purposes.
