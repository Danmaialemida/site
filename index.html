Common Questions

What is the ESA minimum?

The Employment Standards Act sets the legal floor for termination pay in each province. Your employer must pay at least this — but common law typically entitles you to much more.

What is common law entitlement?

Canadian courts use the Bardal factors (age, service, position, job availability) to set "reasonable notice." This is what you can negotiate or sue for — often 5–15× the ESA minimum.

Should I sign immediately?

No. You almost always have time to consult a lawyer first. Many employment lawyers offer free consultations and work on contingency — no upfront cost.

What are Bardal Factors?

From the 1960 landmark case Bardal v. Globe & Mail: courts weigh your age, years of service, position level, and availability of similar work to determine reasonable notice.
