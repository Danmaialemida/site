Understanding Your Rights

What is ESA Minimum?

The Employment Standards Act sets the legal floor for termination pay in each province. Your employer must pay at least this — but it is usually a small fraction of what you are entitled to under common law.

What is Common Law?

Canadian courts use the Bardal factors (age, length of service, position, job availability) to determine "reasonable notice." This is typically far higher than the ESA minimum, and is what you can negotiate or pursue in court.

Should I sign immediately?

No. Most packages have a deadline, but you almost always have time to consult a lawyer. Many employment lawyers offer free initial consultations and work on contingency — meaning no upfront cost.

What are Bardal Factors?

From the landmark 1960 case Bardal v. Globe & Mail: courts weigh your age, years of service, position level, and availability of comparable work to determine the appropriate notice period.
