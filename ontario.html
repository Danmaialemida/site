Ontario Severance Pay Calculator

Ontario has two separate components: Termination Pay (up to 8 weeks) and Severance Pay (up to 26 weeks). Most employees only receive the minimum. Find out what you are actually owed.

Ontario Common Law Entitlements Are Significantly Higher

Ontario courts regularly award 12 to 24 months of pay for long-service, senior, or older employees — far exceeding the 8-week ESA cap. Under the Bardal factors (age, years of service, position, job availability), your common law entitlement can be 5 to 20 times the statutory minimum. Never sign a release without understanding what a court would award.

Ontario Severance Pay — Common Questions

What is the difference between Termination Pay and Severance Pay in Ontario?

Termination Pay is the notice pay every Ontario employee is entitled to (up to 8 weeks). Severance Pay is an additional amount (up to 26 weeks) that only applies when the employer's annual payroll exceeds $2.5 million, making Ontario unique among provinces.

Can I negotiate more than the ESA minimum?

Yes. The ESA sets the legal floor — not the ceiling. Under common law, Ontario courts regularly award notice periods far exceeding the statutory maximum, especially for employees with long service, senior positions, or specialized skills.

How long do I have to accept a severance offer?

There is no fixed deadline, but most employers give you a limited window. You generally have reasonable time to consult a lawyer. Do not sign any release without independent legal advice.

Does Ontario's ESA apply to federally regulated employees?

No. Employees working for banks, airlines, telecoms, and interprovincial transport are regulated by the Canada Labour Code, not Ontario's ESA, even if based in Ontario.
