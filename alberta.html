Alberta Common Law Notice Often Exceeds the 8-Week ESA Maximum

Alberta courts apply the same Bardal factors as other provinces, and commonly award 12 to 24 months of pay for employees with significant tenure, senior roles, or specialized skills. The oil and gas sector in Calgary and Edmonton has produced many cases where long-service employees received substantial common law awards. Get an estimate before you sign anything.

Alberta Severance Pay — Common Questions

Alberta has no ESA severance pay — is that right?

Correct. Unlike Ontario, Alberta only has termination pay under the ESA. However, common law notice in Alberta can be substantial — especially in the energy, tech, and financial sectors where senior roles are common.

What about layoffs in the oil and gas sector?

Employees in Alberta's energy industry are often entitled to significant common law notice, particularly those in senior technical or management roles. Many successful claims have been brought by laid-off energy workers.

Can I negotiate my severance package in Alberta?

Yes. Employers typically offer the ESA minimum as a starting point. With legal advice, many employees successfully negotiate amounts closer to the common law range — sometimes 3 to 10 times the initial offer.

Does just cause dismissal affect my entitlement?

If dismissed for just cause (serious misconduct), you may not be entitled to notice pay. However, employers often claim just cause incorrectly. An employment lawyer can assess whether the grounds are valid.
