New Brunswick Common Law Notice Is Often Far Higher Than the ESA Requirement

New Brunswick courts apply common law principles that regularly produce notice awards well above the statutory minimum. Long-service employees in Fredericton, Moncton, Saint John, and across the province have successfully negotiated or litigated amounts far exceeding their initial offers. New Brunswick Severance Pay — Common Questions

Why does New Brunswick require 6 months of service?

New Brunswick has a higher minimum service threshold than most provinces (6 months vs. 3 months). This means employees who were let go before completing 6 months have no ESA entitlement, though very early dismissal may still have common law implications.

How does the escalating notice work after 5 years?

After 5 years of service, the minimum notice period in New Brunswick increases by 1 week for each additional year served. This means long-service employees accumulate significant entitlements — consult an employment lawyer to confirm your exact amount.

Does New Brunswick require bilingual notice?

New Brunswick is Canada's only officially bilingual province. Employment notices and contracts should be provided in the language of your choice. Always request documents in your preferred language.

Who administers employment standards in New Brunswick?

Post-Secondary Education, Training and Labour administers the New Brunswick Employment Standards Act. Complaints can be filed with the Employment Standards Branch.
