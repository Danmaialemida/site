NWT Common Law Notice Can Substantially Exceed the Statutory Minimum

Courts in the Northwest Territories apply the Bardal factors under Canadian common law. Employees in Yellowknife and across the NWT — particularly those in government, mining, oil and gas, and construction — regularly have strong common law notice claims well above the statutory maximum. Always consult an employment lawyer before signing any termination document.

NWT Severance Pay — Common Questions

Who administers employment standards in the NWT?

The Employment Standards Office within the Department of Education, Culture and Employment administers the NWT Employment Standards Act. Complaints about termination pay can be filed with this office.

Are NWT government employees covered?

Territorial government employees may have different employment conditions governed by collective agreements or separate legislation. Check your employment contract or consult a lawyer familiar with territorial government employment.

What about remote work and fly-in/fly-out arrangements?

The NWT has many employees working on fly-in/fly-out schedules for mining and resource projects. The Employment Standards Act covers these employees in most cases. Consult an employment lawyer for your specific situation.

Is the NWT common law the same as in southern Canada?

Yes. The NWT follows Canadian common law, including the Bardal factors for determining reasonable notice. The same principles that courts in Ontario and BC apply are used by NWT courts.
