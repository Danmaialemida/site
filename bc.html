BC Common Law Notice Can Be Much More Than the ESA Minimum

BC courts routinely award 12 to 24 months of pay under common law, especially for senior employees, long-service workers, and those in specialized roles. The 8-week ESA cap is the legal floor — not a fair settlement. BC's Court of Appeal has consistently upheld significant common law awards. Always consult a lawyer before signing.

BC Severance Pay — Common Questions

Does BC have severance pay in addition to termination pay?

No. Unlike Ontario, BC only has one statutory component — termination pay. However, common law entitlements can far exceed the ESA maximum through negotiation or litigation.

Can I be fired without cause in BC?

Yes — employers can terminate without cause in BC, but they must provide working notice or pay in lieu. The ESA sets minimums; common law sets what is truly reasonable.

What if my employer gave me working notice?

Working notice (remaining at work during the notice period) satisfies the ESA requirement. If notice was inadequate, you may have a wrongful dismissal claim under common law.

Are BC construction workers covered?

Construction industry workers may have different rules under the BC Employment Standards Act. If you work in construction, consult an employment lawyer to confirm your specific entitlements.
